## **3.1 Actuators: The Robotâ€™s Muscles**

Humanoid robots use actuators to **convert energy into motion**. Common types:

| **Actuator Type** | **Description** | **Example** |
|------------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Electric Motors** | Rotational movement with high precision | Servo motors in arms |
| **Hydraulic** | High force & power for legs and torso | Boston Dynamics Atlas legs |

| **Shape Memory Alloys** | Change shape when heated | Micro hand/finger actuation |

## **3.2 Joints & Degrees of Freedom**

- **Joint types**: revolute (rotation), prismatic (linear), spherical (multi-axis)  
- **Degrees of Freedom (DOF)**: Number of independent motions a robot part can perform  

**Example Table of DOF in a humanoid robot:**

| **Robot Part** | **DOF** | **Purpose** |
|----------------|---------|-------------|
| Head | 3 | Pan, tilt, rotation |
| Arms | 7 | Shoulder, elbow, wrist movements |
| Legs | 6 | Hip, knee, ankle |
| Hands | 5-20 | Fingers for grasping |

---

## **3.3 Motion Planning & Trajectory**

Humanoid robots plan motion using algorithms:

| **Step** | **Purpose** |
|----------|-------------|
| **Path Planning** | Determine feasible path in environment |
| **Trajectory Generation** | Smooth motion over time |
| **Collision Avoidance** | Prevent hitting objects/humans |
| **Execution** | Send commands to actuators |

**Tip:** Always test motions in simulation before real robot experiments.

---

## **3.4 Walking & Dynamic Stability**

- **Static walking**: Robot keeps center of mass over support polygon  
- **Dynamic walking**: Robot balances while moving; uses ZMP & CPG algorithms  
- **Fall prevention**: Detect disturbances and recover posture  

---

## **3.5 Arm & Hand Manipulation**

- **Inverse Kinematics (IK)**: Compute joint angles for desired hand position  
- **Grasp Types**: Precision, power, pinch  
- **Compliance**: Soft interactions with humans and objects  

---

## **3.6 Sensors Integration**

- Combine sensors with actuators for real-time feedback  

**Example Table: Sensor Feedback Loop**

| **Sensor** | **Purpose** | **Actuator Reaction** |
|------------|-------------|---------------------|
| Joint Encoder | Position feedback | Adjust motor angle |
| IMU | Orientation/acceleration | Balance control |
| Force Sensor | Contact/pressure | Grip adjustment |
| Camera | Object recognition | Arm trajectory correction |

---

## **3.7 Human Interaction & Safety**

- **Predictable motions**: Robot avoids sudden or fast movements  
- **Force limits**: Actuators respect safe torque levels  
- **Emergency stop**: Software & hardware layers
